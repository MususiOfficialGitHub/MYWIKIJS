Loans

These are the types of loan products being offered to customers. 

Use this sub-module to setup / create new loan products or edit existing loan products. 

Under this setup sub module, you can also attach loan Indefinite Charges that are applicable to a loan product, as well as loan products to be offset and the Appraisal Products to be considered. 

To access this window, click:Accounts Management- > Setup - > Financial Products - > Loans. 

The following window will open up: 

Select a loan product and notice the enlisted tabs below: 

Creating A Loan Product: 

Click on the ADD NEW  Button to open the following screen:

Primary Details Tab:  

 On this Create screen, fill in the following fields. 

Name - Enter the name of the Loan Product

Term (Months) - Enter the term in months (Loan period)

Annual Percentage Rate - Set the annual interest percentage rate by clicking on the arrows pointing up or down

Minimum Principal Amount - Enter the Minimum amount for that Loan Product to be applied for. 

Maximum Principal Amount - Enter the Maximum amount for that Loan Product to be applied

Minimum Membership Period (months) - Enter the minimum period that a customer should have been a member of the Sacco before applying for the loan product.

Grace Period (Days)-Set the appropriate Grace Period (number of days/months the loan should wait before the first recovery) by clicking on the arrow pointing up or down to set the desired grace period for that Loan Product.

Minimum Guarantors - Enter the minimum number of guarantors accepted for that Loan Product

Maximum Guarantees - Enter the maximum number of guarantees  an individual can make for a  Loan Product

Interest Charge Mode - Click on the arrow pointing downwards and select the appropriate Interest charge Mode, this can either be periodic or upfront.

Minimum Chargeable Interest Amount - This is the minimum amount of interest that a member should repay (mostly applies for small amount of loans issued)

Interest recovery mode-Select the appropriate interest recovery mode whether upfront or periodic. Upfront  means interest will be recovered there and then at the point of issuing a loan. Periodic means that the interest will be spread through out the loan period.

Interest Calculation Mode - Click on the arrow pointing downwards and select the appropriate Interest calculation Mode;

  • Amortization(Straight Line) -  the repayment schedule is structured in such a way that the principal and interest are constant through out the repayment period.
  • Amortization(Diminishing Balance) - the repayment schedule is structured in such a way that the principal amount increases through the period while the interest subsequently decreases.
  • Reducing Balance - the repayment schedule is structured in such a way that interest is charged monthly according to the loan balance.
  • Straight Line -  the repayment schedule is structured in such a way that the interest charged is constant while the principal can vary, depending on some conditions.

Section- select the section (Cost center) that the loan product belongs to. (FOSA/BOSA).

Category - Select the appropriate category, short term or long term, according to the product being offered.

Guarantor Security Mode - Select the correct guarantor security mode, either income or investment. 

Aggregate Check-off Recovery Mode - Select the appropriate aggregate check-off recovery mode, either Outstanding balance or Per standing order. 

Payment Due Date- Select the suitable payment due date, either at the beginning of the period or at the end of the period.

Rounding Type - Select the rounding type from the drop down list of options

Rounding Type :

  • Midpoint Away From Zero - if the figure is halfway between two others, it is rounded toward the nearest number that is away from zero.
  • Round Up -  the figure is rounded off to the nearest/ next higher number.
  • Round Down - the figure is rounded off to the nearest/ next lower number.
  • No rounding - no rounding function is carried out on the figure.
  • Midpoint To Even - if the figure is halfway between two others, it is rounded toward the nearest even number.

    

Payment Frequency per year- Select the appropriate Payment Frequency Per Year(how often is the loan paid in a year) by clicking on the arrow pointing downwards. 

Maximum self-guarantee eligible - Type in the maximum self-guarantee eligible percentage. 

Take Home Type - Select the appropriate, whether fixed amount or percentage. 

Take Home Value - Select/ input the appropriate value in accordance with the previously selected take home type, amount for fixed amount and percentage for percentage. 

Payout Recovery Mode - Select whether Per Standing order or outstanding percentage. 

Standing Order Trigger- Select the appropriate standing order trigger either checkoff, micro loan, payout,schedule or sweep. 

Recovery Priority- Set the appropriate Recovery Priority by clicking on the arrow pointing up or down to set the desired recovery priority for that loan Product.

Principal G/L Account - Click on the look up icon to select the G/L Account for the loan product.

Consecutive Income- Set the appropriate Appraisal Factor(The number of times to multiply by the shares/produce) by clicking on the arrow pointing up or down to set the desired appraisal factor multiplier for that Loan Product as per to the Loan policy. 

Interest Receivable G/L Account - Click on the look up icon to select G/L account for interest receivable(Interest Accrued).

Investment Multiplier - Click on the arrows to set the appropriate multiplier or alternatively key in the multiplier figure. 

Interest Received G/L Account - Click on the look up icon to select G/L Account for the interest received(interest paid).

  Conditions Explained: 

  • Subject to Existing-In-Conditional-Lending-List - this implies that a loan will be considered only if the customer has a loan in process.
  • Subject-to-Having-Dividends-Payable - this implies that a loan a loan will be processed only if the customer has payable dividends.
  • Subject to having-fixed-deposits - this implies that a loan will be considered only if the customer has fixed deposits.
  • Subject-to-Having-Loan-In-Process (Appraised)- the loan product will be considered only if the customer has a loan being processed and it has been appraised.
  • Subject-to-Having-Loan-In-Process (Approved)-  the loan product will be considered only if the customer has a loan being processed and and it has been approved.
  • Subject-to-Having-Loan-In-Process (Verified)-  the loan product will be considered only if the customer has a loan being processed and and it has been verified.
  • Subject-to Not-Having-Outstanding-Balance - the loan product will be considered only if the customer has no outstanding balance of any previous loan product.

Loans Products With Auxiliary Investment Appraisal Factors. 


 

               These are the types of loan products being offered to customers with the appraisal factors subject to customer classification. 

               Use this sub-module to create a new loan product or edit an existing loan product to include the auxiliary investment appraisal factors investment ranges and                      corresponding multipliers. 

         

Creating A Loan Product: 


 

Please refer to Loan Products on comprehensive coverage on how to set up a loan product.

Accounts Management   - > Setup - > Financial Products - > Loans > Loan Products With Auxiliary Investment Appraisal Factors.
