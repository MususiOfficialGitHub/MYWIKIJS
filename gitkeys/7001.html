Asset Types 


 

The asset types module is used for categorizing the different types of assets the organization has and capturing them into the system.


 

 Each type of asset has different life spans and depreciation patterns. 


 

 Asset types can be created based on depreciation, useful life span or consumable time frame.

 

These asset types will later be used to classify your organization's assets.


 

 These classifications are used to aggregate assets into different blocks on the balance sheet, so that one can discern the relative liquidity of the assets of an organization.

To access this sub module click on: Procurement and Assets Management -> Navigate -> Setup -> Asset Types.

On the above figure : 

Name - Enter the name of the of the asset type.


 

Depreciation - Select a depreciation pattern for the asset from the drop down menu :         

 

Asset Types Figure 3.


 

  • Fixed-Declining Balance - Where computation of depreciation is done at a fixed rate.
  • Double-Declining Balance - Where the assets depreciates at a higher rate during the initial years of it's useful life, Laptops for example which are more productive when they're new.
  • Straight Line -  Where the value of the asset depreciates at a fixed amount for every accounting period until the end of it's useful life.
  • Sum-Of-Years' Digits - This is also an accelerated depreciation method where a higher expense is incurred in the early years and a lower expense in the latter years of the asset's useful life.
  • Variable Double-Declining Balance - This is a combination of declining balance and straight line.
